# Analytics

Charts: delivery, errors, mix of conversation types, agent stats. Useful when you stop treating WhatsApp as a black box.

> TIP: Pick **one number** you’ll actually act on this month (e.g. error rate or median first reply). More KPIs rarely means more decisions.

---

## What you get

- **Delivery / error** trends over time.
- **Conversation mix** by category where available.
- **Agent** throughput or response metrics (depends on plan).

Exports and scheduled emails vary by workspace — check what’s enabled for you.

---

## When to open this

- After any **big send** — sanity check before the second wave.
- When **leadership asks** “is WhatsApp worth it?” — bring charts, not vibes.

---

## Quick workflow

1. Open the **dashboard** landing on the default range (often last 7 days).
2. Zoom to the **campaign day** you care about.
3. Read **errors** first — creative second.
4. Export **CSV** only if someone will open the file.

> RECOMMENDATION: Screenshot the one chart you discussed in the meeting. Future-you will forget the filter settings.

---

## Common mistakes

- **Comparing weeks with different sends** — apples to oranges.
- **Ignoring denominator** — rates without volume lie politely.

---

## Example

**Tuesday 10:12** you pushed **18,400** marketing messages. By **10:45** the dashboard shows **2.8%** errors — almost all **`131026`** invalid numbers from a recycled list. You export the error bucket, clean the sheet, **don’t** resend to those rows, and the **Thursday** send drops errors under **0.4%**. Same creative; different hygiene.
