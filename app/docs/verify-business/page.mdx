# Verify your business

Complete Meta’s business verification so your WABA, display name, and throughput limits match what your company actually is.

![Verification — documents and business info](/docs/onboarding/step-7.png)

![Verification — Meta review](/docs/onboarding/step-8.png)

## When to use

Use this after WhatsApp is connected but Meta still shows warnings about business verification, or before you request higher messaging limits.

## Steps

<StepBlock>

1. In **Meta Business Suite**, open **Business settings** for the same portfolio you connected to Notifyy.
2. Under **Security**, start **Business verification** and submit legal name, address, and registration documents that match **exactly** (including punctuation).
3. Add a **contact email and phone** Meta can reach — missed callbacks are a common stall.
4. Wait for status **Verified** or follow the **action required** notes Meta leaves on the case. This can take a few business days.
5. Return to Notifyy and confirm there are no business warnings on **Settings → WhatsApp**.

</StepBlock>

## Notes

<WarningBox>
  A mismatch between your storefront brand and your legal entity name is a frequent rejection reason — fix documents instead of resubmitting the same PDF repeatedly.
</WarningBox>

<TipBox>
  You can skip full verification temporarily for some test sends, but do not plan large campaigns on a WABA that still shows a hard business warning — throughput and template review both suffer.
</TipBox>

If verification fails with a generic error, read Meta’s note, fix the single issue they mention, and re-upload once — not five variants at once.

## Example

A retailer submits GST registration and a utility bill for the registered address; verification clears in **3** days and their display name request for the storefront brand is approved the same week.
