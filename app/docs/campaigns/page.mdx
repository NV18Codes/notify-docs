# WhatsApp Campaigns

WhatsApp Campaigns in Notifyy help you send targeted messages to contacts and segments using approved WhatsApp templates.  
Use campaigns when you want to announce offers, send reminders, share updates, promote events, or run repeat communication at scale.

Before creating a campaign, make sure two things are ready: an **approved WhatsApp template** and a clear **audience segment or contact list**. Without an approved template, the campaign cannot be launched.

---

## When should you use campaigns?

Use campaigns when you need to reach many customers with one controlled message flow. A campaign is useful for planned communication where the message, audience, and send time need to be reviewed before delivery.

Common use cases include:

- Sending offers or promotions to selected customers.
- Notifying customers about product, service, or account updates.
- Re-engaging inactive users with a relevant message.
- Sending reminders for payments, appointments, renewals, or events.
- Running recurring outreach such as newsletters or festival greetings.

---

## Campaign Flow Overview

Campaign creation follows a guided wizard. Complete each step in order so Notifyy can validate your sender, audience, schedule, and final message before launch.

<DocsVideo src="/docs/campaigns/campaign.mp4" />

Think of the flow as:

**Template → Audience → Review → Send → Track**

1. **Campaign**: Add campaign details, sender, and template.
2. **Select Audience**: Choose segments or individual contacts.
3. **Schedule**: Decide when the campaign should run and whether retries are needed.
4. **Review & Save**: Confirm all details and create the campaign.

After creation, the campaign appears in the campaign list. If the campaign is scheduled or waiting to run, you can review its status from the list before delivery starts.

---

## 01. Prerequisites

Before you open the campaign wizard, prepare the items below.

- Create and approve a WhatsApp template from **Engagement → WhatsApp Templates**.
- Create audience groups from your contacts or segments.
- Confirm the WhatsApp sender number is connected and has enough daily message limit.
- Keep campaign content, offer details, and send timing ready.

<TipBox>
  Campaign setup becomes faster when templates and segments are prepared first. This also reduces the chance of selecting the wrong audience or sending an incomplete message.
</TipBox>

---

## 02. Start a Campaign

Go to **Campaign Management** and click **Create Campaign**.

In the first step, add the internal campaign information:

- **Campaign Name**: Use a clear name your team can recognize later.
- **Campaign Category**: Choose WhatsApp as the campaign channel.
- **Sender Identity**: Select the verified WhatsApp Business number.
- **Message Template**: Pick an approved template.
- **Description**: Add an optional internal note for context.

The sender limit appears automatically after selecting the sender identity. Check this number before moving forward because your audience size should stay within the available messaging limit.

<img src="/docs/campaigns/campaign-form.png" alt="Campaign details form in Notifyy" className="mx-auto rounded-lg border border-gray-200 shadow-sm my-6" />

At this point, your campaign has the sender and message foundation ready. It is not ready to launch until audience, schedule, and review are completed.

---

## 03. Select Audience

The audience step decides who should receive the campaign and who should be excluded.

You can choose your audience in two ways:

1. **Contacts**: Send to selected individual users.
2. **Segments**: Send to filtered groups of users.

Segments are usually better for targeted campaigns because they let you group contacts by conditions such as signup date, city, lifecycle stage, purchase behavior, or previous engagement.

For example, you can send a campaign only to:

- Customers who signed up in the last 7 days.
- Users who did not respond to a previous campaign.
- Contacts from a specific city or branch.
- High-intent leads who match a saved segment.

You can exclude:

- **Don't Send to Segments**: Suppress groups such as unsubscribed users or converted leads.
- **Don't Send to Contacts**: Exclude specific people even if they belong to a selected segment.

At least one segment or contact must be selected before moving ahead. Use exclusions carefully when you want to prevent duplicate or unwanted communication.

<WarningBox>
  Always review the final audience before continuing. Sending to the wrong segment is one of the easiest mistakes to make in campaign workflows.
</WarningBox>

## 04. Schedule Campaign

The schedule step controls when the campaign should run.

You can choose:

- **Send Now**: Launch immediately after final confirmation.
- **Schedule for Later**: Pick a future date and time.
- **Recurring Campaign**: Repeat the campaign on a fixed schedule.

You can also enable retry for undelivered messages. When enabled, Notifyy attempts to resend messages that were not delivered on the first attempt. Use a retry value between **1 and 3** depending on how important delivery is for the campaign.

<img src="/docs/campaigns/schedule_options.png" alt="Campaign schedule options in Notifyy" className="mx-auto rounded-lg border border-gray-200 shadow-sm my-6" />

Use the detailed schedule controls to decide whether the campaign should run once, later, or as a recurring campaign. If retry is enabled, keep the retry count realistic so customers are not contacted too aggressively.

<DocsVideo src="/docs/campaigns/scheduling_options%20with%20details.mp4" />

<TipBox>
  Enable retries for reminders, alerts, and time-sensitive updates. For standard promotions, one or two retry attempts are usually enough.
</TipBox>

---

## 05. Review & Save

The final step shows a complete summary of your campaign.

Review:

- Campaign name and channel
- Sender identity
- Selected template
- Audience and exclusions
- Schedule mode
- Retry settings
- WhatsApp message preview
- Template variables and sample values

This review step is important because it is your last chance to confirm the message before customers receive it. Check the message content, selected audience, template variables, and schedule carefully. This helps avoid sending the wrong message to the wrong customer group.

If anything looks incorrect, go back to the relevant step and fix it before creating the campaign. Once you create the campaign, it is queued. If **Send Now** was selected, delivery can begin immediately.

<img src="/docs/campaigns/preview.png" alt="Campaign review and preview step" className="mx-auto rounded-lg border border-gray-200 shadow-sm my-6" />

After successful creation, Notifyy shows a confirmation message and returns you to the campaign list.

### What happens when you send a campaign?

Once the campaign is created and starts running:

- Messages are delivered to the selected contacts.
- Delivery and read status are tracked where available.
- Failed or undelivered messages may retry automatically if retry is enabled.
- Campaign results become available in insights after execution begins.

## 06. Campaign List and Status

After creation, campaigns are visible in the WhatsApp Campaigns list. Use search, status filters, type filters, and sorting to locate campaigns quickly.

From the campaign list, you can:

- Search campaigns by name or related details.
- Edit campaigns that are still editable.
- Delete campaigns that are no longer needed.
- Open campaign insights to review performance.
- Check recipient-level delivery and execution details.

Use edit options when a campaign still needs changes before it runs. You can update eligible settings, then save the corrected version.

The list view helps you monitor all campaigns in one place. Search and filters make it easier to find a campaign when your workspace has many active or completed sends.

|---|---|---|
| Ready | Campaign is queued and waiting to send. | No action needed unless you want to review it before launch. |

| Failed | Some or all messages could not be delivered. | Check details, fix issues, and recreate or resend if required. |

Campaign insights help you understand what happened after launch. Use them to review delivery progress, recipient status, failures, and overall campaign performance.

### What can you learn from insights?

After sending a campaign, use insights to understand results instead of only checking whether the campaign was created successfully.

You can review:

- Delivery rate
- Read rate
- Customer responses
- Failed or skipped recipients
- Retry and execution behavior

Use these insights to improve future campaigns, refine audience selection, and adjust message timing.

## 07. Recurring Campaigns

Recurring Campaigns are automated WhatsApp campaigns that repeat on a schedule. Set them up once, define when they repeat, and Notifyy handles future sends automatically.

Use recurring campaigns for:

- Birthday or anniversary wishes
- Weekly offers or newsletters
- Monthly payment reminders
- Festival greetings
- Daily tips or recurring community updates

Recurring campaigns are configured inside the same campaign creation flow. On the schedule step, choose **Recurring Campaign** instead of sending immediately or scheduling once.

---

## 08. Recurring Schedule Options

When recurring mode is selected, configure the first send date, time, repeat pattern, and end condition.

| Field | What it controls |
|---|---|
| Start Date | Date of the first send. Future repeats are calculated from this date. |
| Time | Time of day when each occurrence is sent. |
| Repeats | Frequency: Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Yearly, or Custom. |
| Ends | When the campaign stops: Never, after a number of sends, or on an end date. |
| Max Recurrences | Number of times the campaign should run when an occurrence limit is selected. |
| Repeat on | Weekday selection when the repeat type is Weekly. |

After choosing repeat settings, read the schedule summary carefully. It confirms the full recurrence in plain language and helps catch wrong dates, days, or occurrence counts before activation.

---

## 09. Repeat Frequency Examples

### Daily

Use daily campaigns for short, high-frequency communication such as wellness tips, daily offers, or check-in reminders.  
For example, a clinic can send a daily health tip every morning at 8:00 AM to subscribed patients.

### Weekly

Use weekly campaigns when your communication follows a regular weekly rhythm.  
For example, a retail store can send offers every Wednesday and Saturday evening to keep customers engaged before weekends.

### Monthly

Use monthly campaigns for renewals, invoices, subscription reminders, or monthly reports.  
For example, a finance brand can send EMI reminders on the 1st of every month.

### Yearly

Use yearly campaigns for birthdays, anniversaries, and annual festival greetings.  
For example, a boutique can send a birthday discount to each customer every year.

### Custom

Use custom frequency when standard options do not match your cadence.  
For example, an event team can send a countdown message every two weeks until the event date.

## 10. Quick Launch Checklist

Before creating a campaign, verify the following:

- Approved WhatsApp template is selected.
- Sender identity and message limit are checked.
- At least one segment or contact is included.
- Exclusion lists are configured if required.
- Schedule mode is correct.
- Retry count is set only if needed.
- Message preview looks correct.
- Final summary has been reviewed.

## Common Failure Scenarios

Most campaign issues happen because one required setup item is missing or incorrect. Check these before recreating the campaign.

| Issue | Why it happens | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Campaign cannot launch | Template is not approved | Select an approved template or wait for approval. |
| Messages fail | Phone numbers are invalid or missing country codes | Clean the contact list and retry with valid numbers. |
| Sender unavailable | WhatsApp number is not connected or has limits | Reconnect WhatsApp or choose another sender identity. |
| Audience missing | No contact or segment was selected | Add at least one target audience before continuing. |

## Example Scenario

A clothing brand wants to promote a weekend sale.  
The team first creates and approves a marketing template, then builds a segment of customers who purchased in the last 90 days. In the campaign wizard, they select the approved template, choose the customer segment, schedule the campaign for Friday evening, and enable one retry for undelivered messages.

After creating the campaign, they return to the campaign list and confirm the status. Once the campaign runs, they open insights to review delivery, recipient status, and overall performance.
